Table: employees
Schema: HR
Connection: oracle_10

Description:
Employee information and organizational structure.

Columns:
- employee_id (NUMBER, Primary Key): Unique employee identifier
- first_name (VARCHAR2(50), NOT NULL): Employee's first name
- last_name (VARCHAR2(50), NOT NULL): Employee's last name
- email (VARCHAR2(100), UNIQUE): Corporate email address
- phone (VARCHAR2(20)): Contact phone number
- hire_date (DATE, NOT NULL): Date of hiring
- job_title (VARCHAR2(50)): Current job title
- department (VARCHAR2(50)): Department name
- manager_id (NUMBER, Foreign Key → employees.employee_id): Reference to manager
- salary (NUMBER(10,2)): Annual salary
- commission_pct (NUMBER(2,2)): Commission percentage for sales staff
- status (VARCHAR2(20), DEFAULT 'active'): Employment status

Indexes:
- PK_EMPLOYEES on employee_id
- UK_EMPLOYEE_EMAIL on email
- IDX_EMPLOYEE_DEPT on department
- IDX_EMPLOYEE_MANAGER on manager_id

Foreign Keys:
- FK_EMPLOYEE_MANAGER: manager_id → employees.employee_id (self-reference)

Related Tables:
- employees (manager_id self-reference)
- departments (department references)

Example Queries:
-- Get all employees in Sales department
SELECT * FROM employees WHERE department = 'Sales' AND status = 'active';

-- Find employee hierarchy
SELECT e.first_name, e.last_name, m.first_name as manager_first, m.last_name as manager_last
FROM employees e
LEFT JOIN employees m ON e.manager_id = m.employee_id;
